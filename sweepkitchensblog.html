The Problem Nobody in the KBB Industry Talks About

Here's the uncomfortable truth about most kitchen and bathroom retailers: they're running on a leaky bucket. They pour money into marketing at the top — SEO, beautiful brochures, showroom events — and watch it drain out the bottom because the follow-up process breaks down. The design is presented. The quote is sent. The customer says "we'll let you know." The designer moves on to the next walk-in. And that customer gets filed away in EQ Software or a spreadsheet and forgotten forever.

This isn't a small problem. It's endemic. The average showroom closes somewhere between 20% and 30% of its quotes. That means 70–80% of the people who sat in your showroom and wanted to buy from you, didn't. Not necessarily because they went to Howdens or Magnet. Often because nobody was there to have the conversation at the right moment.

Your old quotes aren't dead. They're just waiting for someone to ask the right question at the right time.

Why They Go Cold in the First Place

Understanding why customers go cold is the first step to understanding why reactivation works so well. People don't stop wanting a new kitchen overnight. Life just gets in the way.

  • 01
    The timing wasn't right

    They were genuinely interested, but an unexpected bill came up, planning permission was delayed, or work got hectic. The desire for the new bathroom didn't disappear — their bandwidth did. Six months later, they're ready again.

  • 02
    They went with someone else — and regret it

    Price shoppers often buy cheap and discover exactly why it was cheap. A reactivation message landing six months after they realised a competitor's quote didn't include fitting is extraordinarily well timed.

  • 03
    They needed more time to decide

    A £15,000 kitchen is an emotional, high-ticket decision. People sit on these for months, debating worktops and appliances. They're not gone — they're still thinking.

  • 04
    Nobody followed up properly

    Your designers are busy designing. They give up after one email checking if the client "had any thoughts on the quote." The gap between what showrooms do and what actually closes deals is enormous.

The SWEEP SMS Framework: The "Bump" Sequence

Database reactivation is the systematic process of going back through your historical CRM data — unconverted quotes, brochure downloads, showroom walk-ins — and restarting conversations with the ones most likely to convert now.

It's not a blast email campaign about a "Winter Sale." Done properly, it's a personalised, natural SMS conversation that feels like it came from the designer who drew up their plans. If they don't reply to the first message, our system uses polite, human "bump" messages to gently prompt a response without being annoying.

💬 Real SWEEP Sequence — Unsold Kitchen Quote
Hi Mark — quick one.

You asked us about a new kitchen a while back.

Did you ever get that sorted or are you still looking into it?
Message 1 (Day 1)
Just checking I've got the right number for Mark?
Bump 1 (Day 3)
No worries if not — I'll leave you alone after this.

Just wanted to check if you still needed a quote for the kitchen?
Bump 2 (Day 7)
Sorry! Yes it is. We had to put the extension on hold for a bit but looking to start again next month. Do you still have our CAD drawings on file?
Replied! (Day 7)
We absolutely do. I can pull them up right now. Have the dimensions for the island changed at all or are we sticking to the original plan?

Notice what just happened. A quote that was written off as dead just re-entered the sales process. No new Google Ads spend. No new leads purchased. Just a well-timed, persistent, human sequence asking the right questions.

The Objection Everyone Has — And Why It's Wrong

The most common pushback on database reactivation is the fear of annoying old leads. "They didn't buy the kitchen the first time — won't texting them three times just irritate them?"

This concern misunderstands how the "Bump" sequence works. A natural, polite text saying "No worries if not, I'll leave you alone after this" is incredibly disarming. It proves you are a real human being who respects their time. The opt-out rate on these campaigns is remarkably low, and the response rate is remarkably high.

The leads who don't want to hear from you will tell you. The ones who do — and there are more of them than you think — will surprise you.

There's also the compliance question. UK businesses operating under GDPR and PECR can legally contact past enquirers under legitimate interests, provided the contact is relevant to their original quote and a clear opt-out mechanism is included. Every SWEEP campaign handles this automatically.
