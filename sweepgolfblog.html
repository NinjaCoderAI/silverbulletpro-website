The Problem Nobody at the Club Talks About

Why They Go Cold in the First Place

Understanding why golfers go cold is the first step to understanding why reactivation works so well. People don't stop wanting to play golf overnight. Life just gets in the way.

  • 01
    The timing wasn't right

    They were genuinely interested in joining, but work got hectic, or winter set in and they decided to hold off. The desire to play didn't disappear. Six months later, as spring approaches, they're ready again.

  • 02
    They joined somewhere else — and regret it

    Golfers often join a cheaper local club and quickly discover exactly why it was cheap—poor greens and impossible weekend tee times. A reactivation message landing six months after they made a bad choice is extraordinarily well timed.

  • 03
    They needed more time to decide

    A £1,000+ annual membership is a big financial commitment. People sit on these decisions for months, discussing it with their playing partners. They're not gone — they're still thinking.

  • 04
    Nobody followed up properly

    The Pro Shop and admin team are too busy running the day-to-day operations of the club to chase up old emails. The gap between what golf clubs do and what actually works for sales is enormous.

What Database Reactivation Actually Is

Database reactivation is the systematic process of exporting your historical data from BRS, GolfNow, or ClubV1 — lapsed members, unconverted enquiries, old society bookings — and restarting conversations with the ones most likely to convert now.

It's not a generic email blast about the upcoming Medal competition. Done properly, it's a personalised, natural SMS or WhatsApp conversation that feels like it came directly from the Club Manager. The kind of message you'd send a warm contact, not a corporate newsletter.

💬 Real example — Golf Membership Reactivation
Hi Mark, it's David at the Pro Shop. I'm just reviewing our files — you played a few rounds with us last year and asked about our 5-day membership. Did you ever join a club for this season, or are you still looking?
Delivered 5:12pm

Replied 5:19pm

Notice what just happened. A lead that was sitting in a GolfNow database doing nothing just re-entered the sales process. No new ad spend. No new leads purchased. Just a well-timed, human message asking the right question.

Why SMS and WhatsApp Are the Only Channels That Work

Email open rates for club newsletters sit somewhere between 15% and 25%. For genuinely dormant contacts — people who haven't played in a year — that number drops further. Email is easily ignored or filed into the 'Promotions' tab.

SMS is different. It lands directly in someone's personal messaging space. Open rates consistently sit above 90%, with most messages read within three minutes. There's no spam filter. It's the most direct channel available to any business.

The key is in the tone. A text that reads like a corporate promotion will be dismissed. One that reads like a genuine check-in — "did you ever get that sorted?" — gets replies. The difference is everything.

The Objection Every Committee Has — And Why It's Wrong

The most common pushback we hear from Club Managers is the fear of annoying old visitors. "Won't texting them irritate them and damage the club's prestige?"

This concern misunderstands the approach. A natural, human SMS asking a simple question — "Are you guys planning another society day this summer?" — doesn't feel like spam. It feels like a club that actually values its visitors enough to check in. The response rate is remarkably high, and the opt-out rate is virtually zero.

The golfers who don't want to hear from you will politely say so. The ones who do — and there are more of them than you think — will surprise you.

There's also the compliance question. UK golf clubs operating under GDPR and PECR can legally contact past visitors and enquirers under legitimate interests, provided the contact is relevant to their original enquiry and a clear opt-out is included.
